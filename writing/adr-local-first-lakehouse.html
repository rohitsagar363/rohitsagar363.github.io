ADR 001

Why the public portfolio uses a local-first lakehouse stack

Status: accepted. This ADR explains why the featured portfolio projects use DuckDB and Parquet locally instead of directly exposing managed cloud services in the public repos.

Context

The target roles are senior data engineering and platform positions in healthcare and pharma. The portfolio needs to prove architecture judgment, testing discipline, medallion-style modeling, and business-ready outputs without forcing reviewers to provision cloud accounts, secrets, or vendor-specific infrastructure.

Decision

Public demo projects use Python, Parquet, and DuckDB as the default execution environment, while keeping the data flow, layering, and quality controls aligned with cloud warehouse and lakehouse patterns.

The objective is portability and inspectability first, not perfect production parity.

Why this was chosen

  • Reviewers can run the projects immediately without provisioning infrastructure.
  • The architecture remains legible: bronze landing, silver conformance, gold serving, tests, and quality gates.
  • The repos avoid fake cloud complexity created only for portfolio theater.
  • The same concepts map cleanly to Glue, Databricks, Spark, Redshift, Snowflake, and BigQuery patterns.

Tradeoffs

  • DuckDB does not prove distributed compute or cluster tuning on its own.
  • Managed-service concerns such as IAM, networking, scheduler permissions, and warehouse scaling are abstracted away.
  • Operational signals like tracing, autoscaling, and queue pressure need to be shown elsewhere in the portfolio.

Compensating controls

  1. The website and repo documentation explicitly connect local patterns back to cloud-native equivalents.
  2. Featured projects include architecture diagrams, quality checks, and tests instead of stopping at notebooks.
  3. A separate future infra repo is planned to prove Terraform, environment management, and operational controls.

Result

This approach makes the projects runnable, reviewable, and credible while preserving the architectural shape that matters for senior data engineering evaluation.
