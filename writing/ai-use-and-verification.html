AI use standard

How AI is used and verified in public engineering work

This standard is intended for portfolio repositories where AI may assist with scaffolding, documentation, or exploratory implementation but does not replace engineering judgment.

Where AI may assist

  • README structuring and first-pass documentation drafts
  • initial SQL or transformation scaffolding
  • test-case brainstorming and edge-case enumeration
  • diagram drafting and naming alternatives

What still requires manual engineering review

  • schema design and business-grain decisions
  • quality rules and domain-specific thresholds
  • retry, idempotency, and failure-handling logic
  • security, governance, and cost-sensitive choices

Verification standard

  1. Read every generated artifact line by line before keeping it.
  2. Rewrite domain logic where the generated output is generic or unverifiable.
  3. Add tests and sample-output validation for critical transformations.
  4. Check architecture and naming against the real business problem, not the generated default.

What should appear in repo documentation

Each major repo should eventually include an `AI Use and Verification` section that states what AI touched, what was changed manually, and how correctness was validated.

Rationale

In 2026, hiding AI usage is weaker than documenting it clearly. The stronger signal is controlled use, domain-specific review, and evidence that the final engineering decisions are deliberate.
